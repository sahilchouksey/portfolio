## 5) Anchor Text Guidelines (Conversion + Relevance Safe Mix)

### Target distribution per destination cluster
- 65-75% descriptive partial-match anchors
- 15-25% exact topical anchors
- 10-15% branded/generic anchors

### Allowed anchor patterns by destination

#### To geo hubs
- Descriptive: `AI engineer based in Jabalpur`, `AI engineering services in Madhya Pradesh`
- Exact: `AI engineer in Jabalpur`, `AI engineer in Madhya Pradesh`
- Branded: `Sahil Chouksey in Jabalpur`

#### To service hubs
- Descriptive: `production-ready RAG development`, `LangGraph agent orchestration services`
- Exact: `RAG developer India`, `LangGraph developer India`, `healthcare AI developer India`
- Branded: `work with Sahil on RAG`

#### To proof hubs
- Descriptive: `medical search engine case study`, `4x API latency improvement case study`
- Exact-ish: `healthcare AI case study`
- Branded: `BRIO Health AI project`

#### To conversion hubs
- Descriptive: `discuss your AI project`, `book a project scoping call`
- Generic (limited): `contact me`, `get in touch`

### Anchor hygiene rules
1. Avoid reusing the same exact anchor to the same destination in every article.
2. Keep anchors context-matched to surrounding paragraph intent.
3. Avoid keyword-stuffed anchors longer than 8 words.
4. Limit one exact-match anchor per destination per page section.
5. Place one high-value internal link in the first 35% of content.

## 8) Crawl Depth Targets and IA Constraints

### Crawl depth targets (from homepage)
- Tier-1 hubs (geo/service/pillars/proof): depth 1
- Tier-2 clusters: depth 2
- Tier-3 supporting posts/FAQs: depth 3 max
- No strategic page deeper than depth 3

### Internal inlink minimums
- Tier-1 page: >= 12 internal inlinks
- Tier-2 page: >= 6 internal inlinks
- Tier-3 page: >= 3 internal inlinks

### Orphan control
- Zero orphan URLs among indexed pages
- Every new URL must be linked from:
  1. one hub page
  2. one sibling page
  3. one navigation/index surface (blog index, related links module, or footer resource block)

### URL and canonical rules
- Keep one canonical URL per topic
- Avoid fragment-only index targets for strategic content (`#Experience`, `#Blog`) once route pages exist
- Include all indexable routes in XML sitemap with valid `<lastmod>`
